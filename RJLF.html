My work in UX design

RJ Leonard Foundation

Objective: Design user experience of RJ Leonard Foundation's website to improve usability, storytelling and visual appeal

"What is the problem?"

Website Audit:

Navigating the current site at length, I identified the following areas of opportunity:

  • Navigation is a challenge, categories of information seem to be in disparate places
  • The story of the foundation is in text form, no visuals
  • The current page is dark and media is black and white, perhaps invoking the wrong message

"How do we solve it?"

Competitor Analysis

From looking at websites like NRDC.org, VOA.org, and Amnesty.org, I found some common elements that make a good non-profit organization website:

  • Images are large, bringing the reader in and captivating them; the images tell the story
  • There is a constant call to action on the top navigation bar and spread throughout the site in easy-to-click button formatting
  • There is a clear message communicated through the categories of "Who we are", "What we do", and "Why it matters"

Site Mapping / Information Architechture Analysis

This mapping shows there are too many clicks needed to access all the information on the site. Instead of each topic on seperate pages, the information should be accessible on longer-format but fewer pages, minimizing the obigation for the user to click continuously through pages to get a holistic view of the foundation's mission and message.

"Let's try this."

With the new goals in mind, I parsed the current site's content and layout and created a wireframe mockup to guide the design of the new site (grey areas denotate images and media):

[Download wireframe in PDF format]

This new layout meets client goals and customer needs in a more elegant fashion:

  • Navigation is simple, the information architecture is more organized under the categories of What, Who, and Why
  • Story of the foundation is told through graphics and media in an easy-to-discover format
  • Page follows modern convention, including full-width images and bright, optimistic visual language
  • Reduces the amount of clicks required to glean the important information from the site
