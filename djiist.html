The Djiist

Objective: Add to existing news aggregator site to improve usability and social functions

The developer of djiist.com approached me and asked if I would help improve his site's user experience

This project helped me learn how to suggest and implement improvements to an existing platform without redesigning the entire site and how to adapt an existing desktop site to work on mobile platforms.

Strategy Meeting

The Djiist is a news aggregator application that pulls information from Google Trends and displays it in a consumable format, using a photo and a short blurb for the content of each "news item". It is still in development, and at this stage the site could benefit from some UX design insight.

To start the project, I met with the developer of the site, who is the sole stakeholder, to discuss the project goals:

  • Goals:

    The client wanted to enhance the experience of his site by adding social functionality and by optimizing the user journey. He also wanted to adapt the platform for easy use on mobile interfaces.

  • Budget:

    I would be doing this project pro bono to gain valuable experience in the UX field.

  • Timeline:

    The client had an undefined timeline. Since he would be performing the back-end development, he wanted to complete the design phase first, within a few months.

  • Vision:

    The client's vision of the site is a place where people go to learn the top news in a short amount of time and be able to get a bird's-eye view of the world.

Competitor Analysis

To gain some insight into how I would improve a news aggregator site, I decided to use other news sites and determine what their strengths are and how their best features are implemented.

To see how other news aggregators work, I took a look at sites like Feedly, Digg, and Reddit:

From browsing these sites I learned:

  • Stories should be modular, self-contained
  • Site should have a hierarchy of information: start small, let user click to expand to learn more
  • Let user customize the experience
  • Use the layout to the advantage of the experience, let users see the story at a glance

Site Usability Audit

In order to see where the opportunities for improvement were on the existing site, I used the site extensively with the following questions in mind:

  • Does the site have enough content keep me interested? How soon will I bounce to another site?
  • What role could social media play in the functionality of the site?
  • What do I feel is missing from the experience?

During my audit I learned:

  • The Djiist bounces me away to external sites quickly and often.
  • Social content could enhance the story by giving a perspective of real online users, but it must remain secondary to news content.
  • More frequent updates of stories could keep users on the site longer.

Site Map/User Journey

To illustrate the current user journey within the site, I created a site map which shows the most likely path of most users.

The site map shows several opportunities for improvement:

  • Create more steps within site before the user gets the chance to visit an external site. The harder it is to get to an external page, the better.
  • Offer more features to process news like favorites, bookmarks, custom feeds based on preferences.

Lo-Fi Wireframing/UI Design

Using Balsamiq Mockups, I sketched out some ideas for the application, keeping the following in mind:

  • Keep users on the site.
  • Add more customization, functionality.
  • Implement social content in an accessible but unobtrusive way.

First, I designed the mobile version.

This follows the design mentality of "Mobile First", which ensures that a desktop version of a platform doesn't become so complex that it can't be implemented onto mobile platforms later.

This design shows:

  • Stories are clickable elements, and when clicked, they expand to a larger view of the story. This will keep users from going to external links so often.
  • There is social content included in each story line-item, shown as a ticker along the bottom of the story.
  • I also imagined a few customizable features, like linking social accounts and being able to "star" a story to save it for later.

Next I mocked up the desktop version:

  • Users get a "ticker" along the bottom of the page (desktop) and along the bottom of each story (mobile) to enrich the experience and show what others are saying about each story
  • If users click on a story, they get a more verbose description along with more robust social media information
  • The desktop mockup shows what it might look like if each expanded story also shows the story from other news sites if they wish to continue to an external site
